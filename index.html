Question 1

A study was performed to investigate the shear strength of soil \((y)\) as it related to depth in feet \((x_1)\) and the moisture \((x_2)\). Ten observations were collected and the following summary quantities obtained:

\[\begin{equation*} n=10, \sum x_{i1} = 123, \sum x_{i2} = 453, \sum x^2_{i1} = 4200.9, \sum x^2_{i2} = 21729, \\ \sum x_{i1}x^2_{i2} = 12252, \sum y_{i1} = 1816, \sum x_{i1}y_i = 43450.8, \sum x_{i2}y_i = 104636.8 \end {equation*}\]

Set up the least squares normal equation and the matrix form for the model: \(y = \beta_0 + \beta_1 x_1 + \beta_1 x_2 + \epsilon\)

Estimate the parameters in the above model.

Solution

In the question above, the variables in the normal equation of the regression model have already been given

\[\begin{align*} \sum y_i & = n\beta_0 +\beta_1\sum x_{i1} + \beta_2\sum x_{i2} \\ \sum x_{i1}y_i & = x_{i1}\beta_0 +\beta_1\sum x_{i1}^2 + \beta_2\sum x_{i1}x_{i2} \\ \sum x_{i2}y_i & = x_{i2}\beta_0 +\beta_1\sum x_{i1}x_{i2} + \beta_2\sum x_{i2}^2 \end{align*}\]

So now we need to write the above systems of equations in matrix form for easy evaluation

\[\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix} n & \sum x_{i1} & \sum x_{i2} \\ \sum x_{i1} & \sum x_{i1}^2 & \sum x_{i1}x_{i2}\\ \sum x_{i2} & \sum x_{i1}x_{i2} & \sum x_{i2}^2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} \beta_0\\ \beta_1\\ \beta_2\\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} \sum y_i \\ \sum x_{i1}y_i \\ \sum x_{i2}y_i \end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}\]
\[\begin{equation*} \Rightarrow \begin{pmatrix} \beta_0\\ \beta_1\\ \beta_2\\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} n & \sum x_{i1} & \sum x_{i2} \\ \sum x_{i1} & \sum x_{i1}^2 & \sum x_{i1}x_{i2}\\ \sum x_{i2} & \sum x_{i1}x_{i2} & \sum x_{i2}^2 \end{pmatrix}^{-1} \begin{pmatrix} \sum y_i \\ \sum x_{i1}y_i \\ \sum x_{i2}y_i \end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}\]

Substituting the necessary values

\[\begin{align*} \Rightarrow \begin{pmatrix} \beta_0\\ \beta_1\\ \beta_2\\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 10 & 123 & 453 \\ 123 & 4200.9 & 12252 \\ 453 & 12252 & 21729 \end{pmatrix}^{-1} \begin{pmatrix} 1816\\ 43450.8\\ 104636.8\\ \end{pmatrix} \end{align*}\]
\[\begin{equation*} \Rightarrow \begin{pmatrix} \beta_0\\ \beta_1\\ \beta_2\\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0.142 & -0.00695 & 0.000957 \\ -0.00695 & -0.0000292 & 0.000161 \\ 0.000957 & 0.000161 & -0.0000649 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 1816\\ 43450.8\\ 104636.8\\ \end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}\]
\[\begin{equation*} \Rightarrow \begin{pmatrix} \beta_0\\ \beta_1\\ \beta_2\\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 56.176\\ 2.995\\ 1.955\\ \end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}\]

Therefore, the correct model for the equation is \(y = 56.176 + 2.995x_1 + 1.995x_2 + \epsilon\)

Solution

In this case, the first step is to linearise the non-linear regression form by taking the natural log of both sides of the equation

\(\Rightarrow \ln y_1 = \ln\beta_0 + \beta_1x_1 + \beta_2x_2\)

\[\begin{align*} \sum \ln y_1 & = \ln\beta_0 +\beta_1\sum x_{1} + \beta_2\sum x_{2} \\ \sum x_{1}y_i & = \ln\beta_0\sum x_1 +\beta_1\sum x_{1}^2 + \beta_2\sum x_{1}x_{2} \\ \sum x_{2}y_i & = \ln\beta_0\sum x_2 +\beta_1\sum x_1x_2 + \beta_2\sum x_{2}^2 \end{align*}\]

Therefore, the model estimate is \(\Rightarrow y_i = 836.310 e^{0.026x_{i1} - 0.001x_{i2}}\)

Question 4

  1. Derive the estimators of \(\beta_0 \ ,\beta_1 \ , \text{and} \ \sigma^2 \quad\) using the method of maximum likelihood. Assuming "\(n\)" random variables are jointly independent and each \(y_i\) is a normal random variable \(y_i \sim NIID(\beta_0 + \beta_1 x_i; \quad \sigma^2)\)

  2. Complete the ANOVA table and test statistical significance of the regression coefficient at \(\alpha = 5\%\)

where \(F_{tab}(0.05) = 3.89\)

Solution

  1. \(y_i = \beta_i + \beta_1x_i + e_i\)

The regression model is:

\[ y_i = \beta_0 + \beta_1 x_i + \epsilon_i \]

Assume:

\[ y_i \mid x_i \sim N(\beta_0 + \beta_1 x_i, \sigma^2) \]

The density function is:

\[ f(y_i \mid x_i, \beta, \sigma^2) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma^2}} \exp\left( -\frac{1}{2} \frac{(y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_i)^2}{\sigma^2} \right) \]

Since the observations are independent, the joint likelihood is:

\[ L(\beta,\sigma^2) = \prod_{i=1}^{n} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma^2}} \exp\left( -\frac{1}{2} \frac{(y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_i)^2}{\sigma^2} \right) \]

This simplifies to:

\[ L(\beta,\sigma^2) = \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma^2}}\right)^n \exp\left( -\frac{1}{2\sigma^2} \sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_i)^2 \right) \]

Taking logs:

\[\begin{align*} L(\beta,\sigma^2) &= \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma^2}}\right)^n \exp\left( -\frac{1}{2\sigma^2} \sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_i)^2 \right) \\ \\ \log L(\beta,\sigma^2) &= -\frac{n}{2} \log(2\pi\sigma^2) - \frac{1}{2\sigma^2} \sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_i)^2 \end{align*}\]

Looks familiar, doesn't it?

To derive \(\sigma^2\), we need to evaluate \(\frac{\delta\log(\beta,\sigma^2)}{\delta\sigma^2}\) and equate it to \(0\)

\[\begin{align*} \Rightarrow \frac{\delta\log(\beta,\sigma^2)}{\delta\sigma^2} & = \frac{-n}{2} \cdot \frac{4\pi\sigma}{2\pi\sigma^2} -\frac{1}{2} \sum (y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_i)^2 \cdot \left(\frac{-2}{\sigma^3} \right) \\ \Rightarrow \frac{\delta\log(\beta,\sigma^2)}{\delta\sigma^2} & = -\frac{n}{\sigma} + \sum \frac{(y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_i)^2 }{\sigma^3} \end{align*}\]

Equating to \(0\):

\[\begin{align*} \Rightarrow -\frac{n}{\sigma} + \sum \frac{(y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_i)^2}{\sigma^3} = 0 \\ \Rightarrow \frac{-n\sigma^2 + \sum (y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_i)^2}{\sigma^3} = 0 \\ \Rightarrow -n\sigma^2 + \sum (y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_i)^2 = 0 \\ \end{align*}\]
\[\begin{align*} \Rightarrow -n\sigma^2 & = -\sum (y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_i)^2 \\ \Rightarrow \sigma^2 & = \frac{-\sum (y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_i)^2}{-n} \\ \Rightarrow \sigma^2 & = \frac{\sum (y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_i)^2}{n} \\ \end{align*}\]
\[ \boxed {\Rightarrow \sigma^2 = \frac{\sum(e_i)^2}{n} } \]

To derive \(\beta_0\), we evaluate \(\frac{\delta\log(\beta,\sigma^2)}{\delta\beta_0}\) and equate it to \(0\)

\[ \frac{\delta\log(\beta,\sigma^2)}{\delta\beta_0} = 0 - \frac{1}{2\sigma^2} \cdot \sum {(y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1x_i)}^2 \]
\[ \frac{\delta\log(\beta,\sigma^2)}{\delta\beta_0} = -\frac{1}{2\sigma^2} \cdot 2\sum {(y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1x_i)}\cdot -1 \]
\[ \frac{\delta\log(\beta,\sigma^2)}{\delta\beta_0} = -\frac{1}{\sigma^2} \cdot\sum {(y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1x_i)}\cdot -1 \]
\[ \frac{\delta\log(\beta,\sigma^2)}{\delta\beta_0} = -\frac{1}{\sigma^2} \cdot\sum {(-y_i + \beta_0 + \beta_1x_i)} \]

Equating to \(0\)

\[ -\frac{1}{\sigma^2} \cdot\sum {(-y_i + \beta_0 + \beta_1x_i)} = 0 \]
\[ \Rightarrow \sum {(-y_i + \beta_0 + \beta_1x_i)} = 0 \]
\[\begin{align*} \sum -y_i + \sum\beta_0 + \sum\beta_1x_i & = 0 \\ -\sum y_i + n\beta_0 + \beta_1\sum x_i & = 0 \end{align*}\]
\[ \Rightarrow n\beta_0 = \sum y_i - \beta_1\sum x_i \]
\[\begin{align*} n\beta_0 & = \sum y_i - \beta_1\sum x_i \\ \beta_0 & = \frac{\sum y_i}{n} - \beta_1\frac{\sum x_i}{n} \\ \end{align*}\]
\[ \boxed {\Rightarrow \beta_0 = \bar{y} - \beta_1\bar{x}} \]

To derive \(\beta_1\), we evaluate \(\frac{\delta\log(\beta,\sigma^2)}{\delta\beta_0}\) and equate it to \(0\)

\[ \frac{\delta\log(\beta,\sigma^2)}{\delta\beta_1} = 0 - \frac{1}{2\sigma^2} \cdot \sum {(y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1x_i)}^2 \]
\[ \frac{\delta\log(\beta,\sigma^2)}{\delta\beta_1} = -\frac{1}{2\sigma^2} \cdot 2\sum {(y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1x_i)}\cdot -x_i \]
\[ \frac{\delta\log(\beta,\sigma^2)}{\delta\beta_1} = -\frac{1}{2\sigma^2} \cdot 2\sum {(-x_iy_i + x_i\beta_0 + \beta_1x_i^2)} \]
\[ \frac{\delta\log(\beta,\sigma^2)}{\delta\beta_1} = -\frac{1}{\sigma^2} \cdot \sum (-x_iy_i + x_i\beta_0 + \beta_1x_i^2) \]

Equating to \(0\)

\[ -\frac{1}{\sigma^2} \cdot\sum {(-x_iy_i + x_i\beta_0 + \beta_1x_i^2)} = 0 \]
\[ \sum {(-x_iy_i + x_i\beta_0 + \beta_1x_i^2)} = 0 \]
\[ \sum -x_iy_i + \beta_0\sum x_i + \beta_1\sum x_i^2 = 0 \]
\[ \beta_1 = \frac{\sum x_iy_i - \beta_0\sum x_i}{\sum x_i^2} \]

Recall that:

\[ \beta_0 = \frac{\sum y_i}{n} - \beta_1\frac{\sum x_i}{n} \]

Substituting the value for \(\beta_0\):

\[ \beta_1\sum x_i^2 = {\sum x_iy_i - \left( \frac{\sum y_i}{n} - \beta_1\frac{\sum x_i}{n} \right) \cdot \sum x_i} \]
\[ \beta_1\sum x_i^2 = {\sum x_iy_i - \left( \frac{\sum x_iy_i}{n} - \beta_1\frac{({\sum x_i})^2}{n} \right)} \]
\[ \beta_1\sum x_i^2 = \sum x_iy_i - \left( \frac{\sum x_iy_i}{n} - \beta_1\frac{({\sum x_i})^2}{n} \right) \]
\[ \beta_1\sum x_i^2 = {\sum x_iy_i - \frac{\sum x_iy_i}{n} + \beta_1\frac{({\sum x_i})^2}{n} } \]

Multiply through by \(n\):

\[ \Rightarrow n\beta_1\sum x_i^2 = n\sum x_iy_i - \sum x_iy_i + \beta_1 \left( \sum x_i \right)^2 \]
\[ \Rightarrow n\beta_1\sum x_i^2 - \beta_1 \left( \sum x_i \right)^2 = n\sum x_iy_i - \sum x_iy_i \]
\[ \Rightarrow \beta_1\left( n\sum x_i^2 - \left( \sum x_i \right)^2 \right) = n\sum x_iy_i - \sum x_iy_i \]
\[ \boxed { \Rightarrow \beta_1 = \frac{n\sum x_iy_i - \sum x_iy_i}{ n\sum x_i^2 - \left(\sum x_i \right)^2} } \]
